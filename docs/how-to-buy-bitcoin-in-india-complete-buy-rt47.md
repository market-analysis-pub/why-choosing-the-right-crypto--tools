## KYC Checklist for India

- [x] Aadhaar / PAN Card (mandatory under FATCA)
- [x] Bank Account with IFSC code
- [x] Phone number and email (live verification)
- [x] Address proof (voter ID, passport, or utility bill)
- [x] Income declaration or source of funds (anti-money-laundering)

## Tax Implications (2026 India)

- **Capital Gains Tax**: Short-term (<2 years): as ordinary income; Long-term: 20% with indexation benefit
- **TDS**: 1% deducted by exchanges on sale proceeds (as of 2024 tax code)
- **Reporting**: Schedule FA (Foreign Assets) if held abroad; Crypto treated as virtual digital asset
- **Loss Carry**: Losses can be carried forward 8 years

## Security Best Practices

1. **Exchange Selection**: Choose SAFU-insured platforms (WazirX, CoinDCX, Zebpay)
2. **Withdrawal to Cold Storage**: Move BTC to self-hosted wallet (hardware wallet recommended)
3. **2FA Setup**: Mandatory authenticator app, SMS backup disabled
4. **Avoid P2P for Large Amounts**: Counter-party risk; use exchange escrow
5. **Regulatory Check**: Verify exchange compliance with IAMAI (Internet and Mobile Association of India)

## Payment Methods Ranked by Speed

1. UPI (instant, 0-30 seconds)
2. NEFT/RTGS (1-2 hours, 9 AM–5 PM weekdays)
3. IMPS (30 minutes, 24/7)
4. Debit Card (varies by bank, 10–30 minutes)

## Regulatory Status (2026)

- **RBI Stance**: No explicit ban; virtual digital assets taxed under GST (5% on services)
- **SEBI**: Crypto not classified as security
- **ED/IT**: Anti-money-laundering enforcement active; large transactions flagged
- **GST**: 5% on exchange fees and trading services

## Regional Availability

- **Supported**: All metro and tier-1 cities with full UPI/banking infrastructure
- **Restricted**: Some exchanges block users in Gujarat/certain states due to local compliance
- **P2P Workaround**: Available in all regions (peer-to-peer trades, OTC)
