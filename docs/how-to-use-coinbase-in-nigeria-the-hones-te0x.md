## Payment Methods by Country

### Nigeria
- Bank transfer (SWIFT, local)
- Naira debit card (limited on major exchanges)
- Mobile money (MTN, Airtel via P2P)
- Cash (P2P, local exchanges)

### Kenya
- M-Pesa (Safaricom)
- Bank transfer
- Local exchange cash deposits

### South Africa
- Instant EFT (local bank transfers)
- SWIFT international
- Credit/debit card

## Regulatory & Risk Notes

1. **Nigeria**: CBN restrictions on banks serving crypto exchanges (as of 2024). Onboard Global partnership allows Coinbase access. P2P and local exchanges operate in grey zone.
2. **Kenya**: Crypto legal but unregulated; exchanges operate freely.
3. **South Africa**: Regulated under POCA; exchanges must comply with AML/KYC.
4. **Ghana, Uganda**: Increasingly crypto-friendly; local exchanges proliferating.

## KYC Requirements (Typical)

| Tier | ID Required | Limits | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Email + phone | $500–$2,000 daily | Instant |
| Tier 2 | Government ID (passport/national ID) | $10,000–$50,000 daily | 1–3 days |
| Tier 3 | ID + proof of address + selfie | Unlimited | 3–7 days |

## Verified Alternatives for Nigeria (2026)

- **Onboard Global**: Coinbase's official gateway; requires KYC, supports bank transfer
- **Luno Nigeria**: Native app, fast verification, local support
- **Binance P2P**: Peer-to-peer trading; highest liquidity
- **LocalBitcoins / Paxful**: Decentralized, anonymous, higher spreads
- **Chipper Cash / Yellow Card**: Mobile-first, remittance-integrated

## How to Choose

1. **For liquidity & low fees**: Binance (P2P if KYC strict)
2. **For regulatory safety**: Luno, Kraken
3. **For speed**: Local exchanges, Paxful
4. **For Coinbase-specific tools**: Onboard Global (Nigeria)
