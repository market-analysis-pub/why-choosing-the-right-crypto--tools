## Tax Thresholds & Reporting

- Capital gains on disposal of virtual assets: Subject to personal income tax if trading activity is deemed a "business"
- Reporting threshold: Transactions >HKD 600,000 (AML/CFT) or >8 BTC equivalent
- No VAT/GST on crypto transactions in Hong Kong
- Holding period: No statutory minimum; gains taxed as business income (not capital gains exempt)

## Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Verify exchange's SFC license status at [sfc.hk/license-lookup](https://www.sfc.hk) (check public register)
- [ ] Complete KYC/AML documentation (ID, proof of address, source of funds)
- [ ] Document transaction date, amount, exchange rate, HKD equivalent for tax records
- [ ] Report amounts >HKD 600,000 to Customs & Excise for AML compliance
- [ ] Retain bank statements, exchange receipts for 7 years
- [ ] Consult tax advisor if annual volume >HKD 1M or if deemed "regular trading"

3. HK resident OTC desk (same-day, higher fees)

### Business/Sole Proprietor (>HKD 500k/year)
1. Business bank account transfer (mandatory documentation)
2. Licensed OTC desk with corporate KYC
3. Stablecoin bridge to offshore account (if needed)

## Risks & Considerations

- Unlicensed exchanges (Binance global, unregistered desks) pose AML reporting risk
- Large cash withdrawals may trigger mandatory reporting; use bank transfers
- HK tax authority increasingly scrutinizes crypto trades; keep detailed records
- Exchange rate slippage on large OTC trades (1-3% common)
- Account freezes possible if source-of-funds documentation incomplete
